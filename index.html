Heat Stress Pilot Study

Guidelines and Instructions for Participants

Welcome to the Heat Stress Study

Thank you for participating in our research study! Your involvement will help improve the safety and well-being of people working in high-heat environments.

This study looks at how extreme temperatures affect people like construction, maintenance, and maritime workers by analyzing data collected through smartwatches.

Heat stress happens when the body takes on more heat than it can release. When this happens for too long, it can lead to heat-related illnesses like cramps, exhaustion, or even heat stroke.

We‚Äôll use data like heart rate, skin temperature, and sweat response to understand how heat affects you on the job. All data is anonymous and helps us design better ways to keep people safe in the heat.

Our goal is to identify high-risk conditions and develop smarter strategies to reduce heat-related risks at work.

Equipment Set-Up

Fitbit Sense 2 Setup and Data Collection Instructions

1. Initial Setup

Follow the steps below to pair your Fitbit Sense 2 and enable required permissions.

  • Download the Fitbit app from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android).
  • Create a Fitbit account or log into an existing one.
  • Turn on your Fitbit Sense 2 and follow the in-app instructions to pair it with your phone.
  • Allow all necessary permissions for:
    • Heart Rate
    • EDA (electrodermal activity)
    • Skin Temperature
    • Sleep Tracking

2. Enable Required Settings

  • Sleep & Skin Temperature
    • Open the Fitbit app and go to the Today tab.
    • Tap the pencil/edit icon to customize visible cards.
    • Make sure "Sleep" and "Body Temperature" cards are turned on.
    • Wear the device overnight for at least 3 nights to establish a temperature baseline.
  • EDA & Stress Tracking
    • Navigate to Settings > Stress & Mindfulness in the app.
    • Turn on "Track body responses."
    • You can keep "Body response notifications" off if not needed.
  • Heart Rate & HRV
    • Confirm the Heart Rate card is visible on the Today tab.
    • HRV is tracked automatically overnight when worn during sleep.
  • System Permissions
    • On your phone, ensure the following are enabled:
    • Bluetooth
    • Motion & Fitness Data
    • Background App Refresh (iOS) or Physical Activity Access (Android)
