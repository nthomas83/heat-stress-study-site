Heat Stress Pilot Study

Guidelines and Instructions for Participants

Welcome to the Heat Stress Study

Thank you for participating in our research study! Your involvement will help improve the safety and well-being of people working in high-heat environments.

This study looks at how extreme temperatures affect people like construction, maintenance, and maritime workers by analyzing data collected through smartwatches.

Heat stress happens when the body takes on more heat than it can release. When this happens for too long, it can lead to heat-related illnesses like cramps, exhaustion, or even heat stroke.

Weâ€™ll use data like heart rate, skin temperature, and sweat response to understand how heat affects you on the job. All data is anonymous and helps us design better ways to keep people safe in the heat.

Our goal is to identify high-risk conditions and develop smarter strategies to reduce heat-related risks at work.
